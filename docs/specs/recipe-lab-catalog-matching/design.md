## Problem Statement

CulinAIre Kitchen's wedge is solving **food cost, profitability tracking, and inventory** for working culinary professionals. The culinary world has not received meaningful tech disruption in the past decade — CulinAIre is that disruption.

**Recipe Lab's strategic role:** Chefs by nature are AI-skeptical. Recipe Lab exists to *create interest with technology and build trust*. It's the front door — a creative, fun, low-stakes surface where a chef can play with AI without putting money on the line. Once they trust the AI in the fun room, they walk into the operational rooms (Stock, Purchasing, Costing) and let it touch the P&L.

The $0.00 Catalog matching gap collapses this entire trust funnel:

```
  Chef plays in Recipe Lab          → AI seems impressive, builds trust
  Imports recipe to Menu Item       → trust transferring to ops side
  Sees Total Food Cost: $0.00       → "this AI doesn't actually work"
  Trust collapses                   → goes back to Toast / Marketman
```

The bug isn't just a broken feature. It's the **trust-killing moment** where a chef decides AI in kitchens is hype. Every chef who hits this screen leaves with a story to tell other chefs about why CulinAIre isn't serious.

### The technical seam

Recipe Lab generates ingredient strings in chef-narrative form (`"flat-leaf parsley, leaves only, roughly chopped — measured after picking"`, `"spaghetti, dried, bronze-die"`). The Catalog (`ingredient` table) holds canonical entities (`Parsley`, `Spaghetti`). The auto-link step in `MenuItemFormModal.handleSelectRecipe` calls `ingredientAliasService.matchBulk` with the raw chef-narrative string. None of the 4 match strategies (exact, case-insensitive, alias, substring) find the canonical entity inside the narrative envelope. `menu_item_ingredient.ingredient_id` stays NULL. `resolveUnitCost` returns `"0"`. Total Food Cost: $0.00. Food Cost %: 0.0%.

Every downstream operations module — Costing, Purchasing, Stock Room, Waste Intelligence — depends on accurate `menu_item_ingredient.unit_cost`. With the seam broken, the entire Kitchen Operations lobe shows zeros where money should be.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Prompt-side split (minimal viable)
Change the DB-managed Recipe Lab prompts so the LLM returns `canonicalName` + `descriptor` separately. `matchBulk` runs against `canonicalName`. Smallest diff. New recipes work; existing recipes need re-generation.
- Effort: S (human ~1 day / CC ~30 min)
- Risk: Low
- Reuses: prompts, recipe schema, matchBulk
- Coverage: 4/10 (leaves existing recipes broken)

### Approach B: Defense-in-depth wiring (ideal architecture) — CHOSEN
A + server-side `normalizeIngredientName()` qualifier stripper + new IngredientPicker disambiguation modal for ambiguous (>1 candidate) matches + auto-escalation to `pending_catalog_request` for unmatched (0 candidates) cases.
- Effort: M (human ~3 days / CC ~2 hours)
- Risk: Med (normalizer regression surface — needs eval tests)
- Reuses: ALL existing catalog-spine infrastructure (ingredient_alias, pending_catalog_request, IngredientPickerInline, matchBulk, resolveUnitCost)
- Coverage: 9/10 (closes all 4 gaps end-to-end)

### Approach C: AI-driven match resolution (creative/lateral)
When matchBulk returns ambiguous/unmatched, call LLM with chef-narrative string + Catalog snapshot. LLM picks canonical ingredient_id with confidence; low confidence auto-escalates.
- Effort: M (human ~3 days / CC ~2 hours)
- Risk: High (hallucination in money-domain; latency + cost per save)
- Reuses: aiService, OpenRouter, pending_catalog_request
- Coverage: 7/10

## Design Philosophy (NORTH STAR — applies to every screen this work touches)

**Chefs are not tech-savvy. The product follows Apple's philosophy: simplify processes, let the wiring, backend, and AI do the heavy lifting.**

Concrete implications for the Recipe Lab → Catalog fix:

- **Default to "it just works."** When the chef imports a recipe, ingredients link automatically. Cost appears. No modal. No picker. No "select the right candidate." If the system can guess with 90%+ confidence, it guesses and moves on.
- **Make corrections cheap, not mandatory.** For ambiguous or low-confidence matches, the system still picks the most likely candidate and shows a subtle inline cue ("not quite right? tap to fix"). The chef can override. They are not forced to.
- **Never show the chef plumbing.** Words like "pending catalog request," "disambiguation," "alias resolution" do not appear in chef-facing UI. Those are admin-side artifacts. Chef-facing UI uses warm language: "we're learning this ingredient," "tap if this isn't right."
- **Zero-tech-debt experience for the chef.** No warning triangles on save. No modal that interrupts the flow. The chef should not have to learn what an alias is, what a pending request is, or what a canonical name is. They should just see the cost number appear.
- **Apple-grade onboarding for new ingredients.** When the chef adds something the catalog doesn't know ("yuzu kosho"), the system silently creates a pending request, uses a placeholder cost, and shows the line at zero or estimated cost with a gentle note ("we'll learn this ingredient — admin notified"). It does NOT block the save.

This philosophy rewrites Approach B's surface details — the underlying wiring is the same, but the chef-facing UX must hide it.

## Recommended Approach: B (Defense-in-depth wiring)

### Why
- Matches the chosen wedge (premise #2 revised): if costing IS the wedge, costing must be **complete and trustworthy**, not patched. B closes all four gaps in one stroke.
- Matches premise #3: reuses every piece of existing catalog-spine infrastructure. No new tables. No new services beyond a single `normalizeIngredientName` utility.
- Avoids hallucination risk in a money-domain (premise #1 — owners rely on accuracy).
- Works for existing recipes today (Approach A would orphan them until re-generation).

### Implementation outline (rough — detailed plan goes in CEO + Eng review)

1. **Prompt-side split.** Edit Recipe Lab prompts (DB-managed via Settings → Mobile → Prompts) to return ingredients with structured fields:
   - `canonicalName: string` (the clean Catalog name)
   - `descriptor: string` (prep, qualifiers, narrative)
   - Keep `amount`, `unit`, `note` as today.
   - Update `RecipeOutputSchema` Zod to match.

2. **Server-side normalizer.** Add `packages/server/src/services/ingredientNormalizer.ts`:
   - `normalizeIngredientName(raw: string): string` — strip qualifiers (everything after `,` or `—`), lowercase, trim. Conservative.
   - Wire into the match-bulk path: before `matchBulk` runs, normalize each input. Keep raw string for display.
   - Eval tests against a corpus of chef-narrative strings.

3. **Silent best-guess linking (Apple-style).** In `MenuItemFormModal`, when `match-bulk` returns `status: "ambiguous"`, the system **picks the highest-scoring candidate automatically** and links it. The chef sees a non-zero cost number and a subtle inline cue on that row ("learned" or a small dot). Tapping the cue opens a low-friction "is this right?" surface where the chef can swap to another candidate. Default flow = no interruption.

4. **Silent auto-escalation.** When `match-bulk` returns `status: "unmatched"`, the system silently:
   - Creates a `pending_catalog_request` row with raw string + recipe context + menu_item_id (admin-side; chef never sees this language).
   - Shows the line with an estimated cost (e.g. average cost of similar items in the same category) OR a calm "we're learning this ingredient" indicator with a placeholder. The save never blocks.
   - Admin gets the request in Settings → Catalog (admin UI, not chef UI). HQ approves → creates new `ingredient` + seeds `ingredient_alias` for the raw string.
   - When admin approves, the chef's menu item refreshes silently with the new cost. The chef sees the number improve without doing anything.

5. **Existing-recipe backfill.** One-off script `scripts/backfill-recipe-canonical-names.ts`:
   - For every recipe in `recipe.recipe_data`, run each ingredient's `name` through `normalizeIngredientName`.
   - Stored result becomes the new `canonicalName`; original moves to `descriptor`.
   - Idempotent — safe to re-run.

## Open Questions

- **Name + workflow of the specific third-party chef.** Demand evidence rests on this; needs to be captured to harden Q1.
- **Backfill triggers re-pricing.** Should the backfill auto-fix all $0.00 menu_item_ingredient rows, or surface them for manual review? Auto-fix risks linking the wrong ingredient if the normalizer is wrong.
- **Alias auto-learning.** When the chef picks a disambiguation candidate, do we auto-write `ingredient_alias(raw → chosen)`? Lower friction, but a single wrong pick poisons all future matches for that string. Default: yes, but log the writes so HQ can audit.
- **Drizzle schema drift.** Per [wiki/synthesis/schema-drift-may-2026.md](../../../My%20AI%20Projects/cc-culinaire-kitchen/wiki/synthesis/schema-drift-may-2026.md), `prep_task.recipe_id` has no FK in DB, `knowledge_document.file_path` has dropped-but-not-migrated 18 rows, 5 unique constraints declared but not enforced. None block this work but should be resolved before any new schema change ships.

## Success Criteria

1. **Quantitative:** For a freshly generated Recipe Lab recipe imported to a Menu Item, ≥80% of ingredient rows auto-link to a Catalog item (`ingredient_id` not null). The remaining ≤20% surface ambiguous-match or pending-catalog UX, not silent $0.00.
2. **Quantitative:** For an existing saved recipe (pre-fix), running the backfill script yields ≥80% auto-link rate; the rest land in pending_catalog_request.
3. **Qualitative:** The owner/chef-owner persona looks at a finished Menu Item and sees a non-zero Total Food Cost and Food Cost % within 30 seconds of importing a recipe — no manual ingredient picking required.
4. **Negative criterion:** Zero silent mis-links. Every link the system can't make with high confidence is escalated visibly. The system never picks the wrong ingredient quietly.
