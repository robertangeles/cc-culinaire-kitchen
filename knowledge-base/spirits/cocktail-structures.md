# Cocktail Architecture and Structures

## Overview

Every well-constructed cocktail follows an underlying structural template---a balance of spirit, sweetness, acidity, dilution, and aromatic elements. Understanding these templates allows a bartender to improvise confidently, substituting ingredients while maintaining balance. The four foundational structures are the sour, the old-fashioned, the highball, and the martini/Manhattan family.

## The Sour

The most versatile cocktail family. Built on the tension between sweet and tart, with spirit as the backbone.

**Core ratio**: 2 oz spirit : 3/4 oz citrus : 3/4 oz sweetener

This is the "golden ratio" for sours and applies to the Daiquiri, Whiskey Sour, Margarita, Gimlet, Sidecar, and dozens of variations.

| Cocktail | Spirit (2 oz) | Citrus (3/4 oz) | Sweetener (3/4 oz) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daiquiri | White rum | Lime juice | Simple syrup |
| Whiskey Sour | Bourbon | Lemon juice | Simple syrup |
| Margarita | Tequila blanco | Lime juice | Cointreau/agave |
| Sidecar | Cognac | Lemon juice | Cointreau |
| Gimlet | London dry gin | Lime juice | Simple syrup |

**Technique**: Shake vigorously with ice for 12-15 seconds to achieve proper dilution (approximately 25-30% by volume) and aeration. Double-strain into a coupe glass. Egg white (approximately 1/2 oz) may be added for a Boston Sour texture---reverse dry-shake (shake with ice first, then discard ice and dry-shake) for optimal foam.

**Adjustments**: Sweeter citrus (Meyer lemon, yuzu) requires less sweetener. Overproof spirits may benefit from a slight increase in citrus to 1 oz.

## The Old-Fashioned

The oldest cocktail template: spirit-forward, lightly sweetened, aromatically enhanced with bitters.

**Core ratio**: 2 oz spirit : 1/4 oz sweetener (or 1 sugar cube) : 2-3 dashes bitters

| Cocktail | Spirit | Sweetener | Bitters |
|---|---|---|---|
| Old-Fashioned | Bourbon/rye | Demerara syrup | Angostura |
| Oaxaca Old-Fashioned | Mezcal/tequila | Agave syrup | Mole bitters |
| Rum Old-Fashioned | Aged rum | Demerara syrup | Angostura |
| Brandy Old-Fashioned | Brandy | Sugar cube | Angostura |

**Technique**: Stir with ice in a mixing glass for 30-40 rotations (approximately 20 seconds). Strain over a single large ice cube in a rocks glass. Express an orange or lemon peel over the surface for aromatic oils. Stirring (not shaking) maintains clarity and a silky, viscous texture appropriate for spirit-forward drinks.

**Sweetener note**: Rich simple syrup (2:1 sugar to water) or demerara syrup is preferred over standard 1:1 simple syrup. The higher viscosity integrates more smoothly with the spirit and requires less volume, preserving the drink's strength.

## The Highball

Spirit lengthened with a carbonated or non-carbonated mixer. The simplest structure, yet highly dependent on ingredient quality and proportion.

**Core ratio**: 1.5-2 oz spirit : 4-5 oz mixer

| Cocktail | Spirit | Mixer |
|---|---|---|
| Gin & Tonic | London dry gin | Tonic water |
| Whisky Highball | Japanese whisky | Soda water |
| Dark & Stormy | Gosling's Black Seal rum | Ginger beer |
| Paloma | Tequila blanco | Grapefruit soda |
| Aperol Spritz | Aperol (3 oz) | Prosecco (3 oz) + soda (1 oz) |

**Technique**: Build in a tall Collins or highball glass over ice. Pour spirit first, then mixer. Stir gently once or twice---excessive stirring disperses carbonation. Ice quality is critical; use dense, clear ice that melts slowly to prevent rapid dilution.

## The Martini / Manhattan Family

Spirit combined with a fortified wine (vermouth) and often bitters. These are aromatic, stirred cocktails where proportion defines character.

**Core ratio**: 2 oz spirit : 1 oz vermouth (adjustable from 3:1 to 1:1)

| Cocktail | Spirit | Vermouth | Accent |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dry Martini | London dry gin | Dry vermouth | Olive / lemon twist |
| Vodka Martini | Vodka | Dry vermouth | Lemon twist |
| Manhattan | Rye whiskey | Sweet vermouth | 2 dashes Angostura, cherry |
| Negroni | Gin (1 oz) | Sweet vermouth (1 oz) | Campari (1 oz), equal parts |
| Rob Roy | Scotch | Sweet vermouth | Angostura |
| Boulevardier | Bourbon | Sweet vermouth | Campari (equal parts) |

**Vermouth freshness**: Vermouth is a perishable wine product. Once opened, refrigerate and consume within 4-6 weeks. Oxidized vermouth is the single most common cause of poor-quality Martinis and Manhattans in bar programs.

**Technique**: Stir with ice for 30-40 rotations. Strain into a chilled coupe (up) or over ice in a rocks glass. Stirring preserves the silky texture and clarity that define this family.
